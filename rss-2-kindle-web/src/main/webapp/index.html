About

RSS-2-KINDLE is a free of charge and absolutely noncommercial service that allows you to transfer content of your RSS-feeds to your favorite kindle device. All you need is just SIGN UP, create a subscriber for your kindle and set up a list of RSS-feeds for it.

The service is provided "AS IS" without warranty of any kind. It is an open source project and code is available on github

Why you need it

  • Do you love your little kindle?
  • Do you you feel boring during commute but always forget to upload new articles into your kindle?
  • Would you enjoy reading your favourite feeds in kindle?
  • Do you have several kindle devices and would like to have different subscription for each of them?
This service is for you
  • Automation of everyday routine
    The service everyday will check updates on your RSS-feed and transform them into kindle format (MOBI) and send them by email to your kindle device.
  • Unlimited number of subscribers
    That means you can associate different subscribers with different kindle devices. For example, somtimes you read books in kindle, somtemies in ipad or iphone. Each of those devices has own kindle-email. You can create a subscriber for each of them. And of cause, you can manage a list of subscriptions for each subscriber independently.
  • Pretty view
    Updates from every RSS-feed will be packaged as one book. So you will easily understand from where a new book came.
  • Flexibility
    If you prefer to manage emails in your own way then you can set up any email for subscriber, not only email of your kindle device.

How it works

  • Step 1: Register your Kindle to Amazon account
    Probably you are already aware that any Kindle device can be registered to Amazon account to buy, download, or deliver content to your device. After the registration a specific "Send to Kindle Email Address" will be assigned to your Kindle. You can check it in settings of your kindle. Email should look like xxxx@kindle.com.
  • Step 2: Add rss-2-kindle to approved e-mail list in Amazon account
    Due to security reason, it is not allowed to send messages to Kindle email from any email. You have to add trusted emails into approved e-mail list in your Amazon account. So, if you wish to receive updates from your RSS-feeds directly to your Kindle using rss-2-kindle service, you need to add the email rss2kindle@service.com into approved e-male list. rss2kindle@service.com is the email that we are using to send updates to your subscribers.
  • Step 3: Create a subscriber in rss-2-kindle account
    Well done. Now you can start working with your rss-2-kindle account. It will be pretty easy and quick. Select "Subscribers" in the navigation tree and select "Add new subscriber".
  • Step 4: Create a list of subscriptions for the subscriber
    It is just list of URLs of RSS-feeds
  • Step 5:
    And vualya - every 24 hours the service will be checking updates in your feeds, convert them into mobi format and send them into subscriber's email

Privacy and security

  • We do not require any sensible information of you.
  • We keep only information provided by you to get a service from us. In particular:
    • Your contact email required for registration
    • Emails of your subscribers to send subscriptions to them
    • URL-s of subscriptions
  • We do not analyse browser cookies and do not analyse content of subscriptions.
  • Your data is secured and not distributed to third party.
